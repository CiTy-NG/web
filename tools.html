Tools and software

Some tools for digital-political emancipation

Mapping Digital Access and Demographic Interactions in LA

This mapping tool visualizes how digital access—both internet and computers—interacts with demographic variables in LA County. It serves as an introduction to data justice issues, addressing historical and current concerns about equitable representation and digital infrastructure. The LA Data Justice Hub aims to build on this tool to explore and address data inequities and opportunities for justice.

Veriform [prototype] - Verifyable, Anonymous Petition Signing Program

Anonymous yet verifiable petition-signing platform inspired by UCLA graduate students who felt unable to openly share their opinions on protests due to fears of institutional or popular backlash. By leveraging a unique token and QR verification process, Veriform ensures that every signature is both legitimate and traceable—without revealing personal information. Its local file storage architecture prioritizes privacy, while the system’s built-in identity checks guarantee each signer is a unique member of the UCLA community. This combination of anonymity and verifiability empowers users to safely and confidently participate in collective decisions and advocacy.

Own Thy Face [prototype] - Crowdsourced mapping tool to support and incentivize reaprpriation of perdonal data

OTF is a GIS-powered web app that incentivizes LA residents to crowdsource CCTV camera locations and photo evidence. Users earn tokens by documenting and verifying surveillance infrastructure, and the platform automatically generates formal letters—addressed to the relevant providers, companies, or city officials—requesting acknowledgment and reappropriation of captured personal data. By aligning with GDPR and CCPA guidelines, this system empowers community members to better understand and advocate for their privacy rights.
