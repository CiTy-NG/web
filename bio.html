Bio

Who I Am

Scholar and Researcher

I am a scholar and researcher in Social Data Science and Information Studies, dedicated to exploring the intersections of technology, society, and governance. My primary research interests include Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW), Decentralized eGovernance, Human-Computer Interaction, and Computational Social Science, with a particular focus on political agency within socio-technical systems.

Academic Journey

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Information Studies at UCLA under the supervision of Ramesh Srinivasan at the Digital Cultures Lab. I hold an MSc in Social Data Science from the University of Copenhagen, where my thesis analyzed the practices of 'decentralized governance' within blockchain ecosystems. My academic journey began at Cornell University, where I earned a BA in Performance-Media Arts with minors in Anthropology and Comparative Literature, graduating Magna Cum Laude.

Mellon Mays Fellow

As a Mellon Mays Undergraduate Fellow, I received foundational training in cross-disciplinary research that continues to shape my work. Most recently, I was awarded the Mellon Graduate Studies Enhancement Grant, which supports my focus on fairness, agency, and accountability in socio-technical systems. In alignment with these values, I currently contribute as a co-author of the LA Data Justice Hub, fostering equitable approaches to data governance.

Interdisciplinary Expertise

In addition to my academic pursuits, I bring interdisciplinary expertise from roles in NGOs, think tanks, and private industry research hubs, where I have applied computational and anthropological methodologies to address real-world challenges in decentralized governance and data justice.

Current Work

Innovative Metrics for DAOs

My current research investigates the socio-technical dynamics of decentralized governance, particularly in Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs), with the aim of designing innovative metrics that better capture political decentralization. Utilizing Natural Language Processing, Network Analysis, and agent-based modeling, I analyze participation and representation dynamics to inform more equitable governance systems.

Leveraging Disagreement

I am also developing tools that treat political antagonism as a valuable asset in decision-making, rather than a challenge to avoid, contributing to frameworks that foster efficient collective intelligence and innovation in digital governance. These efforts align with my broader commitment to advancing sustainable, accountable, and participatory governance models, both online and offline.
