WHO I AM

RESEARCH INTERESTS
My research centers on how digital cooperation and decision-support systems shape political alignment and conflict, guiding how people coordinate, disagree, and negotiate belonging in decentralized or algorithmically mediated settings. My work is informed by conversations in knowledge infrastructures, computational social science, HCI, and CSCW concerning how digital systems structure collaboration and political agency.
ACADEMIA
I am currently pursuing a PhD in Information Studies at UCLA under the supervision of Ramesh Srinivasan at the Digital Cultures Lab. I hold an MSc in Social Data Science from the University of Copenhagen, where my thesis analyzed the practices of 'decentralized governance' within blockchain ecosystems. My academic journey began at Cornell University, where I earned a BA in Performance-Media Arts with minors in Anthropology and Comparative Literature as a Mellon Mays Undergraduate Fellow.

CURRENT WORK

INNOVATIVE METRICS FOR DAOS
My current research investigates the socio-technical dynamics of decentralized governance, particularly in Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs), with the aim of designing innovative metrics that better capture political decentralization. Utilizing Natural Language Processing, Network Analysis, and agent-based modeling, I analyze participation and representation dynamics to inform more equitable governance systems.
LEVERAGING DISAGREEMENT
I am also developing tools that treat political antagonism as a valuable asset in decision-making, contributing to frameworks that foster efficient collective intelligence and innovation in digital governance. These efforts align with my broader commitment to advancing sustainable, accountable, and participatory governance models, both online and offline.
